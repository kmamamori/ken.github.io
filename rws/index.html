Rhetorical Analysis

According to the article, Defining the Heisei Era: the fragmentation of the family in Japan, talks about how poverty or amount of salary is affecting the families in Japan. After the bubble economy collapsed, Japanese family started to have many kinds of issues which led to divorce in many cases. However, many people think Japan as one of the richest countries and all the people in Japan live wealthy as well. In this essay, I will introduce data and facts of how people in Japan are facing poverty with explanation of rhetorical appeals.


On the article, it shows how modern family divorce rate has changed significantly and single rate (not married) has grown this past 30 years. The article stated, \‚Äú80 percent of 20-40 years-old live with, and financially reliant on, their parents\‚Äù. This sentence shows that the majority of the youths cannot afford themselves and have to depend on their parent since the salary is low. I compared two cities, one from Japan called Saitama ( my hometown ) and another from El Paso, Texas through a website called, NUMBEO, which allows to compare cities in terms of: cost of living, property prices, crime, health care, pollution, traffic, quality of life, and travel. According to the website NUMBEO, it tells us that El Paso is lower in consumer prices including rent and groceries prices for over 41% than Saitama, ad restaurant prices is lower than Saitama for 18.67%. However, the average monthly net salary in El Paso is greater than Saitama for 15.82%. The website also states that, \‚ÄúYou would need around $2,449.50 in El Paso, TX to maintain the same standard of life that you have with $4,163.09 in Saitama.\‚Äù This demonstrates that living in Saitama (Japan) consumes more money than El Paso but make less money than El Paso. Two sentences with quotes are using rhetorical appeals of logos because it tells the facts, and data. In addition, it gives very interesting facts about other countries by comparing cities. Through this assignment, I learned more about my two hometown cities, which was truly interesting. As well as, analyzing rhetorical appeals helped me to understand this article even more than reading briefly.

Global Issue Report

Corn dog, pizza, French fries, hamburger, and other lunch are easy to recognize as junk food or unhealthy food; however, they still appear on table during school lunch in the United States. One of the problems that should be addressed is the public-school lunch quality problem. According to Revolution Foods, 88 percent of parents, out of 1800 parents, and 66 percent of students, out of 1800 students both agreed that healthier school lunches should be served in school. Unhealthy food should be eliminated in schools and serve healthier lunches because of the following: better academic performance, better behavior, and prevent obesity. To further understand the beneficial aspects of serving healthier lunch in public schools will be explained in more detail below.

Lack of unhealthy meals served in public schools are affecting students to perform well academically. According to Anderson et al.\‚Äôs article, it states that academic performance can be improved with better quality of school lunch. The authors, Anderson Gallagher and Ramirez Ritchie, cited that \‚Äúa school contracts with a healthy lunch company, students at the school score better ‚Ä¶ on average, student test scores are 0.03 to 0.04 standard deviations higher. Not only that, the test score increases are about 40 percent larger for students ‚Ä¶ who are most likely to eat the school lunches\‚Äù. This demonstrates that healthier lunch in school had affected the students in a good way that made them score better on end-of-year academic tests. By giving foods with quality (healthy), made difference with academic performance and it is obvious that quality matters more than quantity. Talking about academic performance, a study published by Gomez-Pinilla, he states an interesting fact regarding kids learning skills. The study insists that foods that are commonly served during lunch, such as burgers, chicken nuggets, and French fries have a similarity. They are loaded with high levels of saturated fats which may impair learning and memorizing skills of kids.

The quality of lunch has an affect toward academic performance as well as learning skills. In addition to this, it also helps to solve behavior problem. In 1985, Schoenthaler published a series of studies on 12 juvenile correctional facilities, including 8076 young offenders. Dietary changes such as remove chemical additives, processed food, and reduced levels of sugar were held on the facilities and the result had positive impacts had happened. At a facility in Virginia, violence declined by 33% and theft dropped by 77%. At Los Angeles County probation detention centers detected a reduction of 44% in bad behavior, and in a probation in northern California found 25% decrease in violence and 42 % declined of horseplay. In both facilities in California, suicide attempts fell by 44%. Public schools and juvenile detention facilities are very distinct institution to compare. However, in a matter of healthiness there is no difference, and the study should apply to any students in public schools. As a result, improving public school lunch can help students to have a better behavior towards their families, teachers, and classmate and diminish to harm themselves.

In recent years, obesity is becoming on of the major and most serious problem among American children. According to the webpage called ‚ÄúFacts About child Nutrition‚Äù states that ‚Äúthe rate of obesity among U.S. children has tripled in the past 30 years. Today, one in five American children is obese.‚Äú Obesity causes serious healthiness problems such as heart disease and diabetes. During the early life such as adolescent age, it determines the future habits. For example, if children learned to prefer and select healthier foods, they will repeat the same experience in the future. On the other hand, children who accustomed in eating junk food, tends to eat similar food. In addition to this, better (healthier) lunch can bring health for the kids. A study held by Florence et al. showed that student with access of healthy food choices have the potential to have better health. The amount of nutrition in healthy food is larger and each nutrition has their own role to work inside of bodies.

In conclusion, changing to healthier lunches is one of the best and necessary modification that needs to be done in all the public schools, which needs to be changed. For example, in Japan, every public school hires a school lunch dietitian to serve healthy lunch for students, as well as for faculties. Also, every month school provides a menu for one month to every student to inform what ingredients are used and the amount of nutrition. In Italy, there is a law that forces public institutions to use both organic and local products. As introduced above, there are many positive impacts on serving better lunches with quality for any students and governments must act on this serious situation for thee children.
